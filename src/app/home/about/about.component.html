Hello there! Nice to meet you!

I’m a front-end developer, working on web and desktop apps in post-brexit UK. I specialise in HTML, CSS and JavaScript for the web, and a variety of different programming languages and interface frameworks for desktop and mobile.

When I’m not lost in a screenful of code, you’ll probably find me drawing, taking photos, reading up on things or amassing the world’s largest collection of small, cute plastic figures.

Didn’t expect the last one, did you?

Early life

I started building websites and small games as a hobby when I was back in senior school back in the late 2000s, and this ignited my passion for exploring the limits of what computers could do—leading me into some interesting situations during my studies. However, due to a lack of expertise in my school for teaching programming, my pursuits in the field were limited to self-study and extracurricular activities.

Asides from tinkering with programming at school, I also nurtured my creative side. I have always had a dominant creative flair, and my grades in artistic studies during school reflected that. I’ve got a whole history of sketchbooks and scribbles in a box at home—some of them were produced as classwork, the rest being paper pads and notebooks I purchased myself for the sole purpose of letting my imagination run free.

My parents helped me nurture my artistic talents by regularly taking me to art museums and festivals. Additionally, due to a relocation of my father’s job to Singapore during my late teen years, I was exposed to Asian art and culture—something I relished and immersed myself in fully. It was in Singapore I started my character art works as a way of exploring Inkscape, a new tool I’d found.

University studies

I enrolled in Nottingham Trent University to study Computer Science in 2008, after some encouragement from my teachers. Here I managed to focus my programming skill set and pick up knowledge of C++, Java and C#, as well as the processes around software development. Additionally, I took part in a couple of programming competitions with my friends—the most notable being the Microsoft X48 GameCamp Birmingham in 2010, where my team placed second with the game “Nom”.

Thanks to the help of the university, I managed to land an internship position at Schlumberger Oilfield Plc. in Abingdon, UK. While there, I built upon my programming knowledge and was introduced to the world of business applications.

My primary internship project was to build a mobile application which allowed users to view real-time data from a drilling rig. During this project, I found that my artistic skill set came in very useful for designing a user interface that was well adapted for the smaller screens of smartphones. I also learnt Objective C to build the application on Apple iOS devices.

After getting my degree, the company hired me to build UI applications based upon the strengths I’d shown during the internship. My first project there was to help shape the design of an existing application, which I modernised while carefully considering the needs of the end users. I brought a consistent experience to the application, which was well received.

Moving forwards

I’ve been improving my UI and UX skill set since, and at the beginning of 2016 I moved to the web application development team, where I currently work as a UI developer. Additionally, I help with the user experience design through techniques such as end user interviews, wireframe mockups and prototyping.

Creating usable software which is a delight to use is no easy task, and I am constantly learning new things to keep up with the industry. I aim to produce software which puts the user first and in direct control, whilst still being powerful and feature rich.
